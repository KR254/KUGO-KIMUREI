EULOGY


Kimulwo Arap Limo was born in 1927 at Terepkitony village of Mwachon sub-location, Torongo, Koibatek sub-county, Baringo district; He was destined for greatness, and his journey was marked by both triumphs and tribulations. Kimulwo was the son of the late Limo Kimagong, the late Talai Limo and the late Teriki Limo, a liniage stepped in rich heritage and tradition. He was brother to the late Elijah Limo, Late Tabarusei Cheloges, the late Johana Limo, late Wesley Kipchebet, late Kabon Cherop, Salina Masing’o, Anne Cheptoo, Vickola Limo, Late Kiprotich Limo, Late Chuma Limo, Late Jennifer Limo, Kabon Milka Rumbas, Samuel Limo and Esther Chesire, cousin of D.K Limo, the late Francis Bett,Edward Cheboiboch, Arap Tendenei(Laikipia) among others.


MARRIEGE


He got married to the lateTaporisei Kobilo and Kimoi Tapkili in 1958 and later held a traditional marriage. They were blessed with 16 children NAMELY: Late Wilson Cheruiyot Mulwo, Simeon (Smith), Rebecca Kipng’oswo, John Kipserem, Grace Maraba, Rev Shem Limo, Rhoda Kapsermet, Meshack Toroitich, Faith Chebii, Rachel Kamaswai, Monica Kemboi, Leah, Shadrack, Franckline, Robinson and Millicent. He was father in law to Mrs Emily Cheruiyot, Mrs Caroline Kemboi, The Late Joshua Kipngoswo, Paul Yego (Kamaraba- Olare), Mrs Mary Shem (Kapkoech - Kapcholoi), David Maswai, Mrs Margaret Meshack ( Kipasgong arap Yator), Jackson Kiprop, Robert Kemboi, Mrs Rose Koech (Kapsiloi - Sigoro), Sheila Biwott (Kap Richard Biwott) Mrs Rodah Frankline (Kap Joseph Barus), Mrs Fancy Robinson (Kap Barsai). Kugo was blessed with 85 grandchildren (Mogochoronic) and 58 great grandchildren.


CHRISTIANITY


Kugo became a Christian during his youth. In Christianity, he engaged in evangelism in Tenges, Sigoro, Kapsabet under (A.I.M.). During this time, he was working for a colonialist by the name Smith, one day whilst at work Mr. Smith slapped him and from thence going forward he stopped attending church. For many years, he did not attend church until the 4th of September 2022 when he received Jesus to be His Lord and Savior. The pastor who witnessed to him the good news was Pastor Wesley Chepkwony and thereafter prayed for him. The pastor continued to disciple him from that day onwards. The time Kugo was to be baptized his health deteriorated.


SOCIAL LIFE


Kugo Kimulwo received formal education in A.I.M. That is upto grade 3 where he learnt how to read, write and speak English. He was a village elder, who worked in white settler farms at Kapsmith and Lugari. He planted Pyrethrum, potatoes, vegetables, did maize farming and produced dairy products. He was a member of Torongo Farmers Association, Mwachon Farmers association.


HEALTH


Kugo enjoyed good health until 2012 when he developed ulcers. In 2020, up until his demise he stayed in Eldoret receiving treatment and going for regular checkups, while staying in the home of one of his sons Rev. Shem Limo. He experienced difficulty in walking and when an x –ray was conducted it revealed that Kugo had growing bones on the spine which were suppressing the nerves. He was given medication and he got well for some time. He became unwell again in 2021 and was taken to MTRH (Eldoret) his blood was tested which revealed that he was anemic. Due to age factor he could not have a blood transfusion, but instead he was blood boosters. In august 2023 he was yet again ill and was admitted at Living room international hospital where he was treated and discharged after about 10 days. After discharge his health did not improve, this was as a result of not feeding well. March 2024, he complained further about loss of appetite, and whenever he ate he became nauseated. On 14/June/2024 at about 11:00 am, Kugo went to be with The Lord.
